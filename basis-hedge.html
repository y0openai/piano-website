What Is a
Basis Hedge?

It's the strategy used by the world's largest financial institutions.
It can bring your risk down to nearly zero.
The name sounds fancy, but the idea is really simple.

STEP 1

Let's start with an everyday example

Think about planning a trip to Europe.
Nobody knows if the exchange rate will go up or down.
So you exchange your money ahead of time.

✈

Before the trip

You exchange USD to EUR early

→
🛡

Even if rates change

You already exchanged, so no worries

💡

That's exactly what 'hedging' is all about.
You lock in certainty now, instead of worrying about the future.

STEP 2

Now let's apply this to crypto

You buy (LONG) and sell (SHORT) the same asset at the same time.
No matter which way the price moves, they cancel each other out.

Total ≈ 0

STEP 3

If profit is zero, why bother?

Great question.
Here's where the concept of Funding Fee comes in.

What is a Funding Fee?

In perpetual futures markets, a fee is exchanged between longs and shorts every 8 hours.
This is called the Funding Fee.

When there are more longs → longs pay shorts
When there are more shorts → shorts pay longs

In a Basis Hedge, the SHORT position is usually the one receiving the Funding Fee.

⚠ Heads up: The Funding Rate changes depending on market conditions. You won't always receive it. If the Funding Rate turns negative, it can actually become a cost.

WHO USES THIS

So, who actually uses this strategy?

Hedge Funds

Citadel · Bridgewater · Two Sigma

They eliminate directional risk and capture spreads through Basis Trades

Investment Banks

Goldman Sachs · JP Morgan · Morgan Stanley

Their derivatives hedge desks manage client asset risk to stay neutral

Market Makers

Jane Street · Wintermute · Jump Trading

They hedge inventory risk while providing liquidity to the market

Basis Hedge (also known as Cash-and-Carry) is one of the oldest arbitrage strategies in financial markets.
